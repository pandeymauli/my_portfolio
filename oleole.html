1. CHALLENGE

Designing a social application that enables people to watch sports together


2. THE PRODUCT

OleOle is a desktop based platform that lets you enjoy live sports matches with your friends, while being away from each other


3. PROCESS OVERVIEW


3.1 COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Our competitive analysis revealed lack of sports-related interactions among existing platforms.

3.2 INTERVIEWS & OBSERVATIONS

To identify user needs, we conducted 5 intervies and 2 observation sessions with sports fans and people who used one or more of the above listed products. Our key insight was that the application should facilitate enagagements similar to what sports fans feel when hanging out together in sports pubs or at each others' places for match viewing.

3.3 PRODUCT FEATURES

Based on the above insights, we ideated, sketched and refined the following features:

  • Faciliate feeling of intimacy by allowing sports fans to create private chat rooms.
  • Enable users to select and flaunt their allegiance through aliases and team colors.
  • No log in required, thereby keeping the user flow straightforward.
  • Emojis and GIFs to allow fans to display their emotions during the match.


3.4 WIREFRAMES

The high-fidelity wireframes were made by Gaurang, who handled UI design for the app. The first set of clickable UI prototype is available here. Few screens from the UI are presented below.




3.5 USER TESTING

The alpha version of the app was tested twice with 2 groups of approx. 15 people each. Participants were invited to join the chat room, and their experiences with the features were noted. The reviews were favorable, and most participants did not face any issues joining the room. However, we observed that 1-2 participants tended to spam the chat window, and this behavior may require moderation. 1 participant also mentioned facing technical issues. These issues would be resolved in next round of development.

4. DEVELOPMENT

The application was developed using Bootstrap, JavaScript and Flask, a Python web-framework. The code is and UI files are available on GitHub, and be used or modified as seen fit.
